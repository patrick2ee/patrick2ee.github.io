Context

By late 2024, Salesforce Marketing Cloud had become a strategic bottleneck. Velocity was slow — email builds took hours to days — channel expansion stalled (no path to modern SMS or push), and our most-used features required heavy engineering dependence on data extensions, automation SQL activities, and manual middleware. Lifecycle was structurally limited by the platform, not by ambition.

For over a year I led my team's end-to-end platform migration to Iterable. The work required executive sign-off, an aligned roadmap across Engineering, Finance, Compliance, and Architecture, and a phased plan that maintained business continuity through cutover.

Approach

The migration ran in four phases across 2025, each gated on the previous. I owned the plan, the cross-functional dependencies, and the proof-of-success methodology used to de-risk every milestone.

Phase 01 · Q1 2025

Platform readiness & resourcing

Finalized vendor scope and pricing, engaged outboard migration partners where helpful, and defined per-journey and per-channel migration plans (Email, SMS, net-new Push, Ringless Voice). Built executive and cross-functional alignment on the cutover sequence.

Phase 02 · Q2 2025

Foundational launch & scale prep

Launched the first production campaign on Iterable. Established the snippet system, Build Process documentation, and QA gates that would carry the entire migration. This phase set the standard everything after it had to meet.

Phase 03 · Q3 — Q4 2025

Migration at scale

Moved 90%+ of journeys to Iterable while maintaining business continuity. Validated performance against SFMC baselines using a parallel-running funnel methodology (HighTouch + LBRC + Funnel Form Submit), with isolated case studies controlling for platform-specific confounding variables.

Phase 04 · Q1 2026

Full cutover & capability expansion

Migrated the remaining journeys, validated Iterable’s AI/optimization features against equivalents for inclusion in our 2026 roadmap, finalized SMS carrier approval, and launched the first push notification program. SFMC fully sunset.

What changed

Capability SFMC (before) Iterable (after)
Email build time Hours to days; engineering-heavy, custom HTML per campaign Under 15 minutes; marketer-led using the snippet library
Audience routing Engineering middleware via data extensions and automation SQL activities Direct Databricks → Iterable via HighTouch — Lifecycle owns the path
Cross-channel reuse Channel-specific journeys; audience logic duplicated Unified audience logic across Email, SMS, and Push
Suppression visibility Opaque “black box” — ~11M users suppressed with limited rationale Data-dictionary-backed and queryable; transparent in audience counts
Link & UTM integrity Manually applied per email; chronic null-UTM bugs in reporting Applied at the template level; null-UTM incidents driven to near zero
Experimentation infra Manual setup, multi-week lead times HighTouch + Growthbook integration; audience-level splits at touchpoint

Outcome

The migration completed on plan in Q1 2026 and the impact is visible across the funnel:

  • HELOC reachable audience expanded 42% after rebuilding reentry criteria on Iterable’s native audience model.
  • In-Payment Repeat Loans: +26% originations per user and a +30% volume lift, after misalignment in the SFMC implementation was resolved during migration.
  • Activation campaigns: bounce rate down ~80%; Open × Click up ~55% — driven by IP separation, data-integrity fixes, and proper bounce handling.
  • Matured Borrower: +18% subscriber volume, with a 21% drop in same-user duplicates after CIM re-entry logic was rebuilt cleanly.
  • ~3 fewer organizational days per new campaign from removing the middleware between Databricks and the CRM.

Beyond performance: the team is no longer engineering-bound on email production. Marketing managers ship campaigns end-to-end, and the cycle-time savings now compound into strategy and experimentation rather than QA. The migration is the foundation the AI-native pipeline (see the Module Library case study) is built on.
