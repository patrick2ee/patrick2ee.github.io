Context

Disqualified applicants are a notoriously difficult market to tap. We had proven across numerous tests and analyses that once disqualified in an initial application, a user was unlikely to qualify again in a subsequent one. But some did, either because their creditworthiness had meaningfully improved or because our underwriting model had. The scale of the audience was the real prize: millions of personal loan applications are disqualified every year.

The opportunity was to find the small, high-intent slice of that population — the ones who would reapply successfully — without burning trust by re-targeting everyone indiscriminately.

Approach

The strategy split into three branches, each tackling a different part of the problem.

Branch 01

Experimental targeting

Integrated new targeting data into the CDP to slice the previously-disqualified audience along experimental vectors like Adverse Action Notice (AAN) codes, cross-channel prescreen data, and behavioral triggers from other messaging surfaces. This gave us the segmentation granularity to test hypotheses about who within the disqualified pool was most likely to reactivate.

Branch 02

Offline prequalification

Partnered with the Machine Learning team to assess the current creditworthiness of previously-disqualified applicants using their original application details and on-site prediction data. When ML flagged an applicant as high-quality, I would enter them into a personalized flow inviting them to reapply. This was crucial, as we measured incrementality for this audience at 90% for applicants older than 6 months. In other words, they were not reapplying on their own without our outreach.

Branch 03

Actionable insights

Worked with the ML explainability team to build personalized messages triggered at the moment of disqualification. Each message gave the applicant actionable next steps based on the model’s interpretation of their reason for DQ — turning a dead end into a path forward. Result: a 6% lift in reapplication rate from just a single marketing touchpoint, or 10K+ reactivations per month.

Outcome

The combined program reactivated 25,000+ previously-dormant applicants, drove $1.2M in revenue from the December experiment alone, and has been generating $300K / month in run-rate revenue since April 2026 — turning a channel everyone had written off into a recurring contributor.
